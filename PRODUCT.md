# Product

## Register

product

## Users

wr3 is for solo developers and 1-5 person crypto teams shipping AI-assisted or quickly assembled smart contracts on BSC, Base, Arbitrum, Ethereum, and Solana. They usually have limited security budget, work inside Telegram and developer chats, and need a fast pre-audit signal before launch, monitoring, or responsible disclosure.

Secondary users are internal reviewers and researchers who validate findings, manage disclosure, and keep the platform legally safe.

## Product Purpose

wr3 provides an AI-assisted smart-contract pre-audit workflow: source ingestion, static analysis, LLM triage, optional PoC/fuzzing boundaries, transparent scoring, reports, alerts, and responsible-disclosure support. Success means users can quickly understand whether a contract is risky, what evidence exists, what is still unproven, and what safe next step to take without mistaking the product for a guaranteed human audit.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, transparent, and safety-first. The product should feel like a focused security operations tool for small teams: calm under pressure, specific in its claims, and careful about legal boundaries.

## Anti-references

Avoid black-box trust badges, pay-to-play security scoring, generic AI-auditor marketing, exploit-first "hack and rescue" framing, and public scam-callout language. The product should not feel like CertiK Skynet opacity, ChainGPT-style low-trust commodity scans, or a casino dashboard with noisy crypto decoration.

## Design Principles

1. Show evidence before confidence: every verdict needs visible limitations, provenance, and next steps.
2. Keep high-risk actions gated: disclosure, PoC, raw outputs, and public visibility must make the safety boundary obvious.
3. Optimize for anxious builders: the main scan and report flows should reduce uncertainty quickly without hiding caveats.
4. Make transparency a product feature: scoring weights, tool status, skipped engines, and legal gates should be inspectable.
5. Prefer operational clarity over spectacle: the interface can have character, but task completion and trust come first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for the web and Telegram Mini App surfaces. The product needs readable contrast for long reports, keyboard-accessible controls for dense review workflows, clear focus states, reduced-motion support, and non-color-only severity/status communication.
